About Us

Welcome to Cephas Music School!


With a burning desire to contribute to the landscape of music education in Singapore and to improve it where possible, Dr Andjelita Chendra took the leap of faith and founded Cephas Music School in 2010.

By adapting unique methodologies from Belgium and with focus on developing the core foundations, every student can expect to excel in both the early and intermediate stages of their education! We are driven to provide our students with the highest quality education, hence our teachers possess minimally a diploma and they are also reviewed by Dr Chendra.

Our in-house exams are judged by external judges to ensure non-bias and to even better prepare students for the external exams. We encourage students to participate in our biannual concerts to showcase their talents and hard work, and to build their confidence. One dedicated for the higher grade and diploma students to further develop their passion; and the other with our prize-giving ceremony in which we give recognition to our students’ achievements and efforts!

So what are you waiting for? Let us embark on your musical journey together!

Meaning of Cephas

The name Cephas is the Aramaic name of St. Peter, which means rock or stone. It was given to him by Jesus, as in John 1:42

And he brought him to Jesus. Jesus looked at him and said, “You are Simon son of John. You will be called Cephas” (which, when translated, is Peter)

In Matthew 16:13 – 18
When Jesus came to the region of Caesarea Philippi, he asked his disciples, “Who do people say the Son of Man is?” They replied, “Some say John the Baptist; others say Elijah; and still others Jeremiah or one of the prophets.” “But what about you?” he asked. “Who do you say I am?” Simon Peter answered, “You are the Messiah, the Son of the living God.” Jesus replied, “Blessed are you, Simon son of Jonah, for this was not revealed to you by flesh and blood, but by my Father in heaven. And I tell you that you are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church, and the gates of Hades will not overcome it.”

Cephas was a man who has been truly touched by the grace of God, as he was forgiven time and again for his many flaws. It is through His grace, impulsive Cephas was changed to be a graceful and a strong and immovable advocate of Christ and the glory of God, as the power of Christ is made perfect in his weakness; Jesus who foresaw this chose him to lead the church.

We have chosen the name Cephas in his stead. To have Christ as our pillar of strength and to have a strong and immovable foundation. Likewise with Cephas who is not perfect, through the glory of God we strive for excellence.

With the same principles, our curriculum focuses on the foundations so that students may be able to go higher and further than ever before.

Meaning of our Logo

The red represents a few things:
1. Passion for music and continuous learning
2. Love for our staff and students
3. Energy in the sense that we are dynamic and proactive to industry changes
4. Determination to strive for excellence


The G clef in the centre represents the area of our expertise: Music
Within the G clef is a subtle cross, which represents that Christ is our center and our pillar of strength, and at the base of the G clef, is the solid rock which the church is built upon; which is synonymous to the name Cephas.

Be it furthering their studies or to seek a deeper appreciation in music, Dr. Zeng’s deep-rooted passion drives her to help aspiring musicians achieve their goals. She finds satisfaction and shares the sense of achievement when her students’ hard work payoff be it attaining a distinction in the ABRSM exam, attaining their Fellowship Degree, or even performing in front of an audience for the very first time.


In her free time she maintains a healthy body and mind by hitting the gym, to declutter and to help her stay ready in leading Cephas.

Ms. Ethel Loh

Ms Ethel Loh has been teaching for the past 40 years. Possessing a degree in Early Childhood Education coupled with many years of experience, she is adept in engaging children! She enjoys blessing her little pianists with the right skills and mindset to overcome their

obstacles as she showers them with love. She strives to help children enjoy and appreciate music through moving, listening, singing, playing and also creating (their own music)! Having fun while learning is important; for that, she believes would go a long way in developing a love for music, especially for the very young!


When not teaching, she devotes her time to the Lord in prayer and worship meetings.
